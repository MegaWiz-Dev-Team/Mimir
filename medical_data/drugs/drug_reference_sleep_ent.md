## 1. SLEEP AIDS — Hypnotics & Sedatives

### 1.1 Melatonin Receptor Agonists

#### Melatonin (เมลาโทนิน)
- **Class**: Hormone / Dietary supplement
- **Mechanism**: MT1 and MT2 receptor agonist in the suprachiasmatic nucleus
- **Indications**: Circadian rhythm sleep disorders, jet lag, insomnia (adjunctive)
- **Dosage**: 0.5-5 mg PO, 30-60 min before bedtime
- **Side Effects**: Drowsiness, headache, dizziness, nausea
- **Pregnancy**: Generally considered safe (limited data)
- **Key Point**: Low-dose (0.5-1mg) may be more effective than high doses for sleep onset

#### Ramelteon (Rozerem)
- **Class**: Selective MT1/MT2 receptor agonist
- **Mechanism**: High affinity for melatonin receptors, no affinity for GABA, opioid, or serotonin receptors
- **Indication**: Insomnia (difficulty with sleep onset)
- **Dosage**: 8 mg PO within 30 minutes of bedtime
- **Side Effects**: Somnolence, dizziness, fatigue
- **Key Point**: No abuse potential — not a controlled substance

---

### 1.2 Benzodiazepine Receptor Agonists (Z-drugs)

#### Zolpidem (Ambien) — ซอลพิเดม
- **Class**: Non-benzodiazepine hypnotic (imidazopyridine)
- **Mechanism**: Selective GABA-A receptor agonist (α1 subunit)
- **Indications**: Short-term treatment of insomnia
- **Dosages**:
  - IR tablet: 5mg (women), 5-10mg (men) — FDA-recommended lower doses for women
  - CR: 6.25mg (women), 6.25-12.5mg (men)
- **Side Effects**: Somnolence, complex sleep behaviors (sleepwalking, sleep-driving), amnesia
- **⚠️ Warnings**: Risk of next-morning impairment; FDA Black Box warning for complex sleep behaviors
- **Schedule**: Schedule IV controlled substance

#### Eszopiclone (Lunesta)
- **Class**: Non-benzodiazepine hypnotic (cyclopyrrolone)
- **Mechanism**: GABA-A receptor agonist
- **Dosage**: 1-3 mg PO at bedtime
- **Side Effects**: Unpleasant taste (dysgeusia), somnolence, dizziness
- **Key Point**: Approved for long-term use (unlike most hypnotics)

---

### 1.3 Orexin Receptor Antagonists (DORAs)

#### Suvorexant (Belsomra)
- **Class**: Dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA)
- **Mechanism**: Blocks wake-promoting orexin-A and orexin-B neuropeptides
- **Dosage**: 10-20 mg PO within 30 minutes of bedtime
- **Side Effects**: Somnolence, headache, dizziness
- **Key Point**: Novel mechanism — may be safer for long-term use

#### Lemborexant (Dayvigo)
- **Class**: DORA
- **Dosage**: 5-10 mg PO at bedtime
- **Key Point**: Newer DORA with favorable pharmacokinetic profile

## 2. WAKEFULNESS-PROMOTING AGENTS

### Modafinil (Provigil) — โมดาฟินิล
- **Class**: CNS stimulant / wakefulness-promoting agent
- **Mechanism**: Not fully understood; inhibits dopamine reuptake, affects norepinephrine, histamine pathways
- **Indications**:
  - Narcolepsy
  - OSA residual sleepiness (adjunct to CPAP)
  - Shift work sleep disorder
- **Dosage**: 200 mg PO once daily (morning)
- **Side Effects**: Headache, nausea, anxiety, insomnia, dizziness
- **⚠️ Warnings**: Stevens-Johnson syndrome (rare), drug interactions with oral contraceptives
- **Schedule**: Schedule IV controlled substance

### Armodafinil (Nuvigil)
- **R-enantiomer** of modafinil — longer half-life
- **Dosage**: 150 mg PO once daily (morning)
- **Key Point**: May provide more sustained wakefulness throughout the day

### Sodium Oxybate (Xyrem) — สำหรับ Narcolepsy
- **Class**: CNS depressant (gamma-hydroxybutyrate / GHB)
- **Mechanism**: GABA-B receptor agonist
- **Indications**: Narcolepsy with cataplexy, excessive daytime sleepiness
- **Dosage**: 4.5-9 g/night divided in 2 doses (at bedtime + 2.5-4 hours later)
- **Side Effects**: Nausea, dizziness, enuresis, somnolence, headache
- **⚠️ REMS**: Restricted distribution due to high abuse potential
- **Key Point**: Only sleep drug that simultaneously improves nighttime sleep AND daytime alertness

## 3. CPAP / PAP DEVICES (OSA)

### Types
| Device | Description | Indication |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| CPAP | Fixed continuous positive pressure | Standard OSA |
| APAP | Auto-titrating pressure | OSA with variable pressure needs |
| BiPAP | Bilevel (higher inspiratory, lower expiratory) | Complex apnea, COPD overlap |
| ASV | Adaptive servo-ventilation | Central/complex sleep apnea |

### Key Pressures
- Typical range: **5-20 cm H₂O**
- Standard starting: **10 cm H₂O** (titrated by sleep study)
- Compliance threshold: **≥4 hours/night for ≥70% of nights** (CMS definition)

### 4.3 Decongestants
- **Pseudoephedrine** (oral) — 60mg q6h or 120mg q12h (SR)
  - ⚠️ Avoid in hypertension, glaucoma, BPH
- **Oxymetazoline** (topical) — 2 sprays/nostril BID, max 3 days
  - ⚠️ Rhinitis medicamentosa with prolonged use
- **Phenylephrine** (oral) — 10mg q4h
  - ⚠️ Questionable efficacy at OTC doses

### 4.5 Ototoxic Drugs — Monitoring Required
| Drug Class | Examples | Effect |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Aminoglycosides | Gentamicin, Tobramycin | Cochlear + vestibular toxicity |
| Loop diuretics | Furosemide | Transient hearing loss (usually reversible) |
| Platinum chemo | Cisplatin | Permanent SNHL (dose-dependent) |
| Salicylates | High-dose aspirin | Tinnitus, reversible hearing loss |
| Macrolides | Azithromycin, Erythromycin | Rare — high doses or renal impairment |
